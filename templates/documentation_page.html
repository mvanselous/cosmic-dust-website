The color is presented as a difference in magnitude between the brightness in red and blue filters, color = -2.5 * log10((brightness_blue / brightness_red)); note that the solar color is not included here, so it should be subtracted from the observational data to fit the modeling results. Positive color is usually called red, and negative is called blue, neutral color is equal to zero. For a mixture of two materials, the resulting color is calculated as color = -2.5 * log10((brightness_blue_1+brightness_blue_2) / brightness_red_1+brightness_red_2)) ,where 1 and 2 mean material 1 and material 2.
